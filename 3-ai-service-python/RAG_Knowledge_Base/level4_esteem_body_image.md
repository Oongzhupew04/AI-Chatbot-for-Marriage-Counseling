# Maslow Level: Esteem Needs
## Sub-Category: Body Image and Physical Changes
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Attractiveness, Respect)

**Psychological Context:**
Esteem is closely tied to how a person views themselves and how they believe their partner views them. Physical changes due to aging, illness, or pregnancy can severely damage a partner's self-esteem. If a partner feels they are no longer attractive to their spouse, their emotional confidence plummets, often leading them to withdraw from physical intimacy entirely.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user mentions feeling "ugly," "old," or insecure about weight gain, or if they suspect their partner is no longer attracted to them, the core issue is an Esteem deficit. The withdrawal from intimacy is a symptom of feeling fundamentally unvalued.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Validate the Vulnerability:** Acknowledge how difficult it is to experience physical changes. Validate that feeling desired is a core human need.
2. **Reframe Attraction:** Advise the couple to expand their definition of attraction beyond purely physical metrics. The partner must explicitly compliment the user's energy, intellect, and non-physical attributes.
3. **Specific, Unprompted Validation:** Advise the spouse to offer unprompted, genuine compliments about the user's physical appearance. Reassurance must be proactive, not just a response to the user's insecurities.
