# Maslow Level: Physical Needs
## Sub-Category: Severe Depression and Postpartum
## Related Survey Variables: Happiness / Daily Functioning

**Psychological Context:**
Severe clinical depression, including postpartum depression, manifests physically. It removes a person's energy, ability to sleep, and basic motivation to survive. When one partner is deeply depressed, the physical and emotional load of the entire marriage falls onto the other partner, mimicking the dynamics of a chronic illness.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user describes a partner who cannot get out of bed, cries constantly, or expresses hopelessness, the AI must recognize this as a medical-grade physical deficit, not just a "sad mood" or a lack of romantic effort.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Medical Evaluation First:** Strongly advise that the depressed partner seek evaluation from a psychiatrist or medical doctor. Therapy and medication are often required to restore a baseline of physical functioning.
2. **Remove Relationship Blame:** Reassure both partners that the depression is a medical intruder, not a reflection of the marriage's worth or the partner's love.
3. **Patience with Anhedonia:** Explain that depression causes "anhedonia" (the inability to feel pleasure). Advise the healthy partner not to take the lack of enthusiasm or intimacy personally during this medical episode.
