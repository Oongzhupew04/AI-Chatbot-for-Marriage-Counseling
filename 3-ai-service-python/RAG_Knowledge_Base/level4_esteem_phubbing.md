# Maslow Level: Esteem Needs
## Sub-Category: Phone Addiction and "Phubbing"
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Enjoying company, Respect)

**Psychological Context:**
"Phubbing" (phone-snubbing) occurs when a partner constantly chooses their screen over engaging with their spouse. In the modern era, attention is the highest currency of respect. Giving priority to a phone over a partner's conversation sends a devastating Esteem signal: "This screen is more important than you."

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user complains that their spouse is always on social media, texting, or playing games during dinner or in bed, they are expressing an Esteem injury. They feel invisible and devalued.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Identify the Core Injury:** Point out that scrolling while a partner is talking is not just a bad habit; it is a micro-rejection that kills emotional intimacy over time.
2. **Create Tech-Free Zones:** Advise the couple to agree on strict "no-screen" zones or times, such as the dinner table or the first 20 minutes in bed, to force intentional eye contact and conversation.
3. **The 10-Minute Check-In:** Suggest the couple implement a daily 10-minute check-in where phones are physically placed in another room, ensuring 100% undivided attention.
