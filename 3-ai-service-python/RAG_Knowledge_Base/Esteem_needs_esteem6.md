# Maslow Level: Estimacy Needs  
## Sub-Category: Esteem Needs  
## Related Survey Variables: esteem6  

**Psychological Context:**  
The question about parents taking pride in their child's accomplishments ties into the Maslow Levels of psychological needs. Specifically, it relates to the Esteem Needs subcategory, which involves the importance of self-worth and self-assurance. Parents' emphasis on their children's achievements can influence a child's sense of estimacy, affecting both personal growth and family dynamics. If parents neglect this aspect, it can lead to strained relationships or poor communication, potentially impacting overall marital health.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**  
The lack of focus on estimacy needs in families can contribute negatively to marital harmony. The parent-child dynamic is crucial for emotional stability. A parent who does not value the child's accomplishments as part of their success may lead to unresolved conflicts due to communication breakdowns. This can impair family relationships and reduce both members' well-being, undermining overall happiness.

**Actionable Advice:**  
1. **Recognize Estimacy Needs:** Identify and address the importance of a child's estimacy by valuing their accomplishments.  
2. **Focus on Strengths:** Recognize that strength is key to estimacy rather than focusing solely on achievements.  
3. **Encourage Open Communication:** Foster trust and communication to rebuild family dynamics, ensuring both parent and child feel valued.
