# Maslow Level: Self-Actualization Needs
## Sub-Category: Meaning, Happiness, and Spiritual Alignment
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Are you happy?), Religiosity (Are you religious?)

**Psychological Context:**
Self-actualization is the pinnacle of the hierarchy, representing personal growth, profound happiness, and shared meaning (often expressed through shared values or religiosity). If lower-level needs are met but a partner still feels unfulfilled, it is a crisis of meaning. Differences in religious intensity (religiosity) can also fracture a couple's shared vision of life.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
When basic marital functioning is fine but "happiness" is absent, or if differing religious values cause conflict, the couple is struggling to self-actualize together. They lack a shared purpose or spiritual alignment.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Finding Shared Meaning:** If happiness is low, advise the couple to explore what gives their life purpose outside of daily chores. Suggest volunteering, taking up a meaningful shared hobby, or setting life goals together.
2. **Navigating Religious Differences:** If differing religiosity is a pain point, advise the couple to focus on shared *core values* (like kindness, charity, or family) rather than theological differences. Teach them to support each other's spiritual or personal growth journeys without demanding conformity.
3. **Individual Growth:** Remind the user that a healthy marriage supports the individual self-actualization of both partners. Encourage them to ask their spouse: "How can I support your personal dreams this year?"
