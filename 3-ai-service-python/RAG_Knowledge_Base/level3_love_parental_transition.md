# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: Transition to Parenthood and Loss of Romance
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Cuddling, Romance) & Number of Children

**Psychological Context:**
The arrival of children drastically shifts a couple's energy. In the Maslow framework, the intense focus on the child's survival (Physical/Safety) often causes the parents to completely neglect their own dyadic bond (Love and Belonging). They transition from romantic partners to purely logistical co-managers of the household.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If parents of young children complain about feeling like "roommates" or feeling unloved, diagnose this as child-centric marital neglect. The romantic foundation is starving due to a lack of dedicated time and attention.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Normalize the Phase:** Reassure the couple that the "roommate phase" is a normal, temporary psychological response to the exhausting early years of parenting.
2. **Micro-Connections:** Since grand romantic gestures are often impossible with kids, advise the couple to focus on 5-minute "micro-dates"—drinking coffee together uninterrupted, a 30-second hug in the kitchen, or checking in emotionally before discussing the kids.
3. **Guard the Marital Boundary:** Encourage the couple to establish firm boundaries around adult time, such as strict bedtimes for children to ensure the parents have at least one hour of guaranteed alone time each night.
