# Maslow Level: Esteem Needs
## Sub-Category: Intellectual Superiority and Condescension
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Respect)

**Psychological Context:**
Esteem is destroyed when one partner acts as if they are intellectually superior to the other. Phrases like "Let me explain this so you understand," or constantly treating the spouse like a child who cannot make financial or logistical decisions, erodes the victim's self-worth and creates a Parent-Child dynamic rather than a Peer-to-Peer marriage.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user feels they are being talked down to, "managed," or treated like an employee, the foundation of mutual respect is completely compromised. The condescending partner often believes they are "just being helpful," masking their need for control.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Identify the Parent-Child Dynamic:** Point out that treating a spouse like a subordinate kills romantic and sexual intimacy. Spouses want to sleep with their equals, not their managers.
2. **Ask, Don't Instruct:** Advise the condescending partner to replace unsolicited advice with curiosity. Instead of saying "You should do it this way," they must learn to ask, "Do you want my advice, or do you just want to vent?"
3. **Restore Decision-Making Power:** Encourage the couple to identify areas of the marriage where the "subordinate" partner can take complete, unquestioned leadership to rebuild their confidence and equal footing.
