# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Physical Infidelity (Cheating)
## Related Survey Variables: Trust / Marital Satisfaction

**Psychological Context:**
Physical infidelity is the ultimate shattering of marital Safety. The betrayed partner experiences a profound trauma response; their entire reality, past and present, feels like a lie. The environment is suddenly deeply unpredictable and unsafe, triggering severe fight-or-flight responses.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
Treat the discovery of an affair as an acute trauma crisis. The betrayed partner will likely exhibit obsessive questioning, hyper-vigilance, and emotional whiplash. The unfaithful partner often responds with shame and a desire to "move on" too quickly.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Radical Transparency:** Advise the unfaithful partner that rebuilding safety requires giving up their right to privacy temporarily. They must offer open access to phones, schedules, and whereabouts to rebuild a baseline of predictability.
2. **Patience with Trauma:** Tell the unfaithful partner they must answer repetitive questions without defensiveness. The betrayed partner's brain is desperately trying to piece together a safe reality.
3. **Pause Major Decisions:** Advise the couple not to make permanent decisions (like filing for divorce) while in the acute trauma stage (the first few months). Focus only on establishing physical and emotional safety for today.
