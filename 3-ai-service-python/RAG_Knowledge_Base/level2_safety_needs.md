# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Future Security and Stability
## Related Survey Variables: Pension / Retirement Security 

**Psychological Context:**
Safety needs involve predictability, stability, and protection from future harm. The survey question regarding reliance on pension and social benefits in old age touches on a couple’s long-term financial safety net. A lack of agreement or confidence in this area creates existential dread about aging and abandonment.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
Disagreements or fears about long-term security (like pensions) reveal a fracture in the marital safety net. Fear of the future often manifests in the present as controlling behaviors or extreme frugality.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **De-escalate Fear:** Recognize that worries about old age and pensions are rooted in a deep human need for safety. 
2. **Proactive Planning:** Encourage the couple to have a "Future Security" meeting. If they strongly disagree about their reliance on pensions, they must map out an alternative, concrete retirement plan.
3. **Reassurance:** Guide the partners to verbally assure each other that they will care for one another as they age, regardless of the financial outcome.
