# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: Companionship, Intimacy, and Extended Family
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Enjoying company, Attractiveness, Cuddling, Romance, Love) & GLOBE (Aging parents/children living at home)

**Psychological Context:**
This level encompasses the core emotional bonds of a marriage. It includes romantic intimacy (enjoying company, finding the partner attractive, cuddling) as well as the couple's integration into the broader family unit (whether aging parents or adult children live at home). Deficits here lead to feelings of profound loneliness, isolation, and roommate-syndrome.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a partner reports "No" to enjoying company, cuddling, or finding the partner attractive, the romantic bond is fracturing. Additionally, cultural disagreements over whether adult children or aging parents should live in the home can put severe strain on the couple's private sense of belonging.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Addressing Intimacy Gaps:** If romantic connection is low, advise small, low-pressure bids for connection. Suggest starting with non-sexual physical touch (like holding hands or cuddling) and scheduling dedicated quality time to "date" each other again.
2. **Navigating Extended Family:** If the issue involves in-laws or adult children living at home, advise the couple to establish clear, unified boundaries. The marriage must be the primary relationship; living arrangements for extended family must be agreed upon jointly.
3. **Reigniting Love:** Suggest the couple actively practice expressing appreciation and physical affection daily to rebuild their foundation of belonging.
