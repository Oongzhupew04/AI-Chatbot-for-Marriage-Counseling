# Maslow Level: Self-Actualization Needs
## Sub-Category: The Empty Nest and Reclaiming Shared Purpose
## Related Survey Variables: Number of Children (Transitioning out of home) / Happiness

**Psychological Context:**
When children finally leave the home, couples often face a profound existential crisis. The shared project of raising children is over. If they haven't maintained their connection, they are left looking at a stranger. This is a Self-Actualization challenge: "Who are we now, and what is our purpose for the next 30 years?"

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If an older couple (or a couple with adult children) reports feeling empty, bored, or disconnected without active conflict, they are struggling to transition into the next phase of life. They need a new shared vision.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Grieve the Transition:** Acknowledge that it is entirely normal to feel a sense of grief and profound loss when children leave, even if it is a successful milestone.
2. **Rediscover the Individual:** Before connecting as a couple, encourage both partners to ask themselves what hobbies, passions, or self-actualization goals they put on hold for the last two decades.
3. **Build the "Next Chapter" Vision:** Advise the couple to brainstorm a new shared purpose. This could involve traveling, volunteering, mentoring, or simply restructuring their daily lives to maximize joy and companionship.
