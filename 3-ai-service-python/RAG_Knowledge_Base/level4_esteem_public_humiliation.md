# Maslow Level: Esteem Needs
## Sub-Category: Public Humiliation and Mockery
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Respect, Pride)

**Psychological Context:**
Esteem involves how we are viewed by our partner, but also how our partner presents us to the world. When one spouse makes "jokes" at the other's expense in front of friends, or openly corrects and belittles them in public, it is a severe violation of Esteem and loyalty. 

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
Disguising contempt as humor is a classic defense mechanism. If a user says, "They claim it was just a joke, but it hurt," diagnose this as passive-aggressive contempt. The partner is chipping away at the user's self-worth.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Call Out the Behavior:** Advise the user to privately but firmly state: "When you make jokes about me in front of our friends, it does not feel funny to me. It feels like a betrayal. You need to stop."
2. **Defend the Spouse in Public:** The AI must teach the standard that a spouse is their partner's biggest public defender. Disagreements must be handled in private, never as public entertainment.
3. **Examine the Resentment:** Suggest the offending partner reflect on why they feel the need to take public "digs" at their spouse, as it usually points to unaddressed, deeper resentments.
