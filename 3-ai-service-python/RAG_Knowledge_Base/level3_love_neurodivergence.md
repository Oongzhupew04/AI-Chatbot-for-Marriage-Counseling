# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: ADHD and Neurodivergence in Marriage
## Related Survey Variables: Communication / Daily Functioning

**Psychological Context:**
When one partner has ADHD or is neurodivergent, standard communication advice often fails. The neurotypical partner may interpret forgetfulness, distraction, or hyper-fixation as a lack of love or respect (destroying Belonging). The neurodivergent partner often feels chronic shame and failure for not meeting standard marital expectations.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user complains that their spouse "never listens," "forgets everything I say," or acts like a distracted child, the AI must consider a neurological mismatch rather than malicious intent. The danger is the "Parent-Child" dynamic forming around daily chores.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Separate Symptom from Intent:** Advise the neurotypical partner to separate the neurological symptom (distraction) from the partner's intent (they still love you). Forgetfulness is not a personal betrayal.
2. **Implement External Systems:** Stop relying on memory. Advise the couple to use shared digital calendars, physical whiteboards, and visual reminders to manage household tasks.
3. **Praise the Effort, Not Just the Outcome:** Advise the couple to actively praise the neurodivergent partner's efforts to manage their symptoms, reducing the heavy burden of chronic shame.
