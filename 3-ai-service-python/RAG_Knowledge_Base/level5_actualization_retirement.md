# Maslow Level: Self-Actualization Needs
## Sub-Category: The Retirement Transition
## Related Survey Variables: Pension Security / Happiness

**Psychological Context:**
Retirement removes a massive pillar of daily identity and purpose. Suddenly, a couple is spending 24 hours a day together without the buffer of work. If they haven't planned for their psychological Self-Actualization post-career, they often drive each other crazy with micromanagement and boredom.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If an older user complains that their retired spouse is "always in the way," micromanaging the house, or deeply depressed, the couple is failing the retirement transition. The spouse has lost their purpose.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Establish Daily Routines:** Advise the couple to treat retirement with the same structure as a job. They must create new daily routines that get them out of the house and out of each other's immediate space.
2. **Define Separate Domains:** If the newly retired partner is micromanaging the household, they need to be assigned a new, specific domain of responsibility that is entirely their own to manage.
3. **Rediscover Individual Purpose:** Encourage the struggling partner to seek out volunteer work, mentoring, or a part-time passion project to rebuild their sense of individual self-worth outside of the marriage.
