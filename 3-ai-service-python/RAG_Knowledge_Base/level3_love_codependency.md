# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: Enmeshment and Social Isolation
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Enjoying company) / Social Support

**Psychological Context:**
While Love and Belonging require deep connection, "codependency" or enmeshment occurs when partners completely lose their individual identities. They rely exclusively on each other for all emotional, social, and psychological needs, cutting off external friendships. This creates a suffocating environment where one partner's bad mood instantly ruins the other's day.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user says, "We do everything together, but I feel trapped," or "I have no friends outside of my spouse," the AI must diagnose a lack of healthy boundaries. True belonging requires two distinct individuals choosing to connect, not two halves trying to make a whole.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Encourage External Support:** Advise both partners to intentionally rebuild friendships and hobbies outside of the marriage. A spouse cannot be expected to be a best friend, therapist, and sole confidant all at once.
2. **Differentiate Emotions:** Teach the user the concept of emotional differentiation: "I can be supportive of your bad day without absorbing it and making it my bad day."
3. **Celebrate Individuality:** Encourage the couple to spend occasional evenings apart doing what they individually love, bringing new energy and stories back into the marital conversation.
