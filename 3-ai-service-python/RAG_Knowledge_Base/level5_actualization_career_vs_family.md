# Maslow Level: Self-Actualization Needs
## Sub-Category: Competing Visions (Career vs. Family)
## Related Survey Variables: GLOBE (Cultural expectations) / Happiness

**Psychological Context:**
Self-actualization requires living in alignment with one's core values. A severe crisis occurs when partners realize they have fundamentally different visions for their lives—for example, one partner wants to sacrifice everything to build a business, while the other wants to prioritize a quiet family life and work-life balance.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
This is not a petty argument; it is a clash of ultimate life goals. If a user feels their partner's career is destroying the family, or if a user feels their partner is holding them back from greatness, their Self-Actualization paths have diverged.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Acknowledge the Divergence:** Validate that it is painful when life visions shift. Neither vision (ambition vs. family focus) is inherently wrong, but the incompatibility is causing pain.
2. **Seek the Compromise Anchor:** Advise the couple to find the "anchor" they both agree on. Can the ambitious partner dedicate 100% to their career for 3 years, with a strict agreement to scale back afterward? 
3. **Define Success Together:** Instruct the couple to explicitly define what a "successful life" looks like for them as a unit, ensuring that individual ambitions do not destroy the shared foundation.
