# Maslow Level: Self-Actualization Needs
## Sub-Category: The Mid-Life Crisis and Identity Shifts
## Related Survey Variables: Age / Happiness

**Psychological Context:**
Often occurring in middle age, a partner may experience an acute existential panic regarding their mortality, leading to a sudden, desperate search for "Self-Actualization." This can result in erratic, out-of-character behavior—buying expensive things, changing careers, or seeking younger company—leaving the spouse feeling abandoned by the person they knew.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
The AI must recognize that a mid-life crisis is an individual identity crisis that spills over into the marriage. The frantic search for meaning is fundamentally a Self-Actualization panic.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Separate Identity from Marriage:** Help the user understand that their partner's crisis is about the partner's fear of aging/death, not necessarily a reflection of their love for the spouse.
2. **Hold Boundaries Without Punishment:** Advise the stable spouse to support their partner's safe explorations (like a new hobby or fitness routine) while holding firm boundaries against destructive behavior (like financial ruin or infidelity).
3. **Re-evaluate Together:** Use this crisis as an opportunity. Ask the couple to sit down and openly discuss: "What are your deepest regrets, and how can we use the next 20 years to make sure you live fully?"
