# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Secret Debt and Financial Infidelity
## Related Survey Variables: Material Status / Trust

**Psychological Context:**
Discovering that a partner has secretly accumulated massive credit card debt, gambled away savings, or hidden bank accounts is categorized clinically as "financial infidelity." It shatters the Safety level just as violently as physical cheating, because it actively threatens the couple's survival (housing, food, future stability).

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
The betrayed partner will feel intense panic, betrayal, and unsafety. The offending partner will likely exhibit extreme shame, defensiveness, or minimization. The AI must treat this as a severe breach of systemic trust.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Total Financial Transparency:** The offending partner must immediately surrender financial privacy. All passwords, bank statements, and credit reports must be accessible to both partners to begin rebuilding a safe reality.
2. **Damage Assessment:** Before addressing the emotional betrayal, the couple must act like a crisis management team. Advise them to sit down and map out the exact, objective number of the debt without arguing.
3. **Rebuild Trust Slowly:** Remind the betrayed partner that it is okay to feel unsafe. Trust is rebuilt through months of verifiable, consistent financial honesty, not through a single apology.
