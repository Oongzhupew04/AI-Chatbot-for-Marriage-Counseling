# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Lying and Gaslighting
## Related Survey Variables: Trust / Respect

**Psychological Context:**
Gaslighting is a form of psychological manipulation where one partner repeatedly denies facts, twists past events, or invalidates the other's memories, causing the victim to doubt their own sanity. This completely destroys cognitive Safety; a person cannot feel secure if they cannot trust their own mind.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user frequently says, "I feel crazy," "Am I remembering this wrong?", or "My partner says I'm too sensitive when I catch them in a lie," the AI must recognize severe psychological unsafety.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Validate Reality:** Firmly validate the user's feelings. If they know a lie was told, tell them to trust their gut. Their reality is valid.
2. **Stop the Debate:** Advise the user to stop arguing over "what really happened." If the partner is gaslighting, arguing feeds the cycle. Advise them to state their truth once ("I know what I saw/heard") and disengage.
3. **Document Reality:** Suggest the user start writing down conversations or agreements in a journal or via text message immediately after they happen to anchor themselves to objective facts.
