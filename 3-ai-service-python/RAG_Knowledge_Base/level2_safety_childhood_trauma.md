# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Projecting Childhood Trauma
## Related Survey Variables: General Safety / Trust

**Psychological Context:**
If a partner grew up in an abusive or chaotic home, their nervous system is wired to expect danger. In marriage, they may project this trauma onto their spouse, perceiving innocent actions (like being late or sighing) as severe threats. This sabotages marital Safety because the traumatized partner is reacting to a ghost from their past, not their current spouse.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user's reaction to a conflict seems wildly disproportionate to the event (e.g., screaming over a spilled glass of water), or if they constantly assume their partner is going to abandon them, unresolved childhood trauma is likely hijacking the present.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Recognize the Trigger:** Advise the traumatized partner to recognize when they are emotionally "flooded." Teach them to ask: "Am I reacting to what my spouse just did, or am I reacting to a feeling from my past?"
2. **De-escalate with Reassurance:** Advise the non-traumatized spouse to use explicit verbal reassurance during conflicts (e.g., "I am angry about the dishes, but I am not leaving you").
3. **Individual Therapy:** Clearly state that while a spouse can be supportive, they cannot heal childhood trauma. The traumatized partner must seek individual therapy to rewire their trauma responses.
