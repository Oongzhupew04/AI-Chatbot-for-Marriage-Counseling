# Maslow Level: Esteem Needs
## Sub-Category: Chronic Criticism and Defensiveness
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Respect, Pride in Spouse)

**Psychological Context:**
Esteem requires mutual respect. Criticism—attacking a partner's character rather than their specific behavior—is toxic to Esteem. Saying "You forgot the trash, you are so lazy" (criticism) is vastly different from "I am frustrated the trash wasn't taken out" (complaint). Criticism inevitably breeds defensiveness.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user says they feel they "can never do anything right," or if they describe their partner as constantly nitpicking, Esteem is actively being destroyed. This dynamic traps the couple in an attack/defend loop.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Complain Without Blame:** Teach the user to reframe criticisms into complaints. Advise using "I feel... about what... and I need..." instead of "You always..." or "You never...".
2. **Drop the Defensiveness:** Advise the partner receiving the feedback to search for the "kernel of truth" in the complaint rather than immediately defending their actions.
3. **The 5-to-1 Ratio:** Remind the couple of the clinical standard: for every one negative interaction or critique, there must be five positive, appreciative interactions to maintain healthy marital Esteem.
