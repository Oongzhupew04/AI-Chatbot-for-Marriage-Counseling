# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: Infertility Struggles and Miscarriage
## Related Survey Variables: Number of Children / Happiness

**Psychological Context:**
The inability to conceive, or the trauma of losing a pregnancy, places a massive strain on Love and Belonging. The couple is grieving a profound loss of their envisioned future. Often, partners grieve differently—one may want to talk about it constantly, while the other withdraws into silence—which creates feelings of intense isolation and fractured belonging.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a couple is struggling with IVF, infertility, or a miscarriage, the AI must recognize this as a shared, chronic trauma. The danger here is that partners turn away from each other because witnessing the other's pain is too difficult.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Validate Different Grieving Styles:** Reassure the couple that there is no "correct" way to grieve. One partner's silence does not mean they do not care; it is simply a different coping mechanism.
2. **Protect the Intimacy:** Infertility treatments often turn physical intimacy into a clinical, stressful chore. Advise the couple to reclaim their bedroom by scheduling intimate time that explicitly does not involve discussions about ovulation or baby-making.
3. **Create a "Grief Boundary":** Suggest setting aside a dedicated 20 minutes a day to openly discuss their sadness, fears, or medical plans. Outside of those 20 minutes, they must focus on being a romantic couple to prevent the trauma from consuming their entire identity.
