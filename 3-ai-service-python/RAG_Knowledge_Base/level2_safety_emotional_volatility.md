# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Emotional Predictability and Anger
## Related Survey Variables: General Safety / Conflict Resolution

**Psychological Context:**
Safety needs are not just physical; they are highly emotional. If one partner has a volatile temper, yells frequently, or gives the silent treatment, the other partner is forced to "walk on eggshells." This destroys the marital environment's predictability, stripping away the foundational sense of Safety.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user expresses anxiety about bringing up issues because they fear their partner's reaction, the marriage lacks emotional safety. The primary emotion here is anxiety and hyper-vigilance. 

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Establish Emotional Safety Rules:** Advise the user that a healthy marriage requires a "No Yelling" and "No Name-Calling" baseline. 
2. **Implement the 20-Minute Time-Out:** Teach the couple the clinical time-out rule. If an argument escalates to yelling, either partner can call a time-out. They must physically separate for 20 minutes to let their nervous systems calm down before resuming the conversation.
3. **Identify Triggers, Not Just Reactions:** Encourage the volatile partner to identify the underlying emotion (usually fear, shame, or feeling unheard) that happens *right before* the anger spikes.
