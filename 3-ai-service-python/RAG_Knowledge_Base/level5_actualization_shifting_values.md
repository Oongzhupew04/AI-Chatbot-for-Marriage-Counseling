# Maslow Level: Self-Actualization Needs
## Sub-Category: Shifting Core Political or Religious Values
## Related Survey Variables: Religiosity / Happiness

**Psychological Context:**
People grow. Over a decade or more, one partner may undergo a profound ideological shift, changing their core political, religious, or moral worldview. Because these values dictate Self-Actualization and meaning, a sudden divergence here can make spouses feel like they are married to an alien.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user complains that their spouse has become "radicalized," left their faith, or fundamentally changed their worldview, the couple is experiencing a crisis of shared meaning. 

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Focus on Underlying Ethics:** Advise the couple to stop debating the specifics of theology or politics and instead look for the shared underlying ethics. (e.g., They may vote differently, but do they both still value kindness, charity, and family?)
2. **Practice Radical Tolerance:** Remind the couple that a marriage can survive differing worldviews if both partners replace the need to "convert" each other with genuine curiosity about *why* the other person believes what they do.
3. **Grieve the Loss:** Validate the user's grief. It is okay to mourn the loss of the shared spiritual or political alignment they used to have, while still choosing to love the person in front of them today.
