# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: Intercultural and Interracial Clashes
## Related Survey Variables: GLOBE (Societal and Cultural values)

**Psychological Context:**
In intercultural marriages, the definition of "Belonging" can be vastly different. One culture may prioritize the nuclear family (just the couple), while another prioritizes the extended collective family (parents, cousins, elders). Disagreements over holidays, money given to extended family, or child-rearing are not just logistical; they are deeply ingrained cultural identities.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user feels overwhelmed by their spouse's family expectations, or feels their own traditions are being erased, the AI must diagnose a cultural belonging clash. The couple has failed to create a unified "third culture."

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Assume Positive Intent:** Remind the couple that cultural differences are not personal attacks. A partner's expectation (e.g., sending money to parents) is rooted in their cultural definition of love, not in trying to hurt the spouse.
2. **Build a "Third Culture":** Advise the couple that they must actively create a new, unique "third culture" for their own marriage, consciously choosing which traditions to keep from each background and which to discard.
3. **Protect the Spouse from Extended Family:** The AI must insist that each partner is responsible for educating their own family about the new marital boundaries, protecting their spouse from cultural criticism.
