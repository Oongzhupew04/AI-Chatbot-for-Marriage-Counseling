# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: In-Law Interference and Boundary Violations
## Related Survey Variables: GLOBE (Aging parents living at home / Cultural expectations)

**Psychological Context:**
Marriage requires shifting one's primary allegiance from the family of origin to the spouse. When parents or in-laws dictate marital decisions, drop in unannounced, or live in the home without healthy boundaries, it fractures the couple's private "Belonging" unit. The spouse feels they are competing for their partner's loyalty.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
Complaints about in-laws are fundamentally complaints about boundaries and loyalty. If a user feels their spouse always sides with their parents, the core issue is that the marital unit is not functioning as the primary team.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **The "Us First" Rule:** The AI must gently advise that the marital bond must take precedence over the family of origin. The partner must protect their spouse from external familial criticism.
2. **Unified Front Communication:** If a boundary needs to be set with an in-law, the biological child of that parent must be the one to communicate it, using "we" language (e.g., "Mom, we have decided that...").
3. **Negotiating Shared Space:** If aging parents live in the home (referencing the GLOBE variable), the couple must collaboratively draft "house rules" that protect their private romantic space and adult autonomy.
