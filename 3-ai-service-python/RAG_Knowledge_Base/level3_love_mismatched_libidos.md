# Maslow Level: Love and Belonging Needs
## Sub-Category: Mismatched Sex Drives
## Related Survey Variables: MRQ (Romance, Attractiveness)

**Psychological Context:**
It is incredibly common for partners to have different levels of desire for physical intimacy. However, over time, the higher-drive partner feels chronically rejected and unloved, while the lower-drive partner feels objectified, pressured, and broken. This turns the bedroom into a battleground rather than a place of Belonging.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
The AI must remain entirely neutral here. Neither partner is "wrong." The issue is not the frequency of sex, but the emotional meaning attached to the rejection and the pressure.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **De-couple Touch from Sex:** Advise the couple to practice physical affection (cuddling, back rubs, holding hands) with a strict agreement that it will *not* lead to sex. This removes the lower-drive partner's anxiety and fulfills the higher-drive partner's need for touch.
2. **Communicate Rejection Gently:** Teach the lower-drive partner to reject the act, not the person. (e.g., "I love you and find you so attractive, but my body is too exhausted right now.")
3. **Explore the "Brakes":** Encourage the couple to discuss what "turns off" the lower-drive partner (stress, mess, feeling unheard) and work as a team to remove those obstacles, rather than just focusing on what "turns them on."
