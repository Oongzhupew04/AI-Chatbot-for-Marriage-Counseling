# Maslow Level: Physical Needs
## Sub-Category: Chronic Illness and Caretaker Fatigue
## Related Survey Variables: General Health / Daily Functioning

**Psychological Context:**
When one partner develops a chronic illness or disability, the marital dynamic often shifts from "partners" to "patient and caretaker." This severely taxes the Physical Needs level. The healthy partner suffers from profound physical exhaustion and caretaker burnout, while the ill partner often struggles with guilt and a loss of physical autonomy.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user is overwhelmed by caring for a sick spouse, or if a sick spouse feels like a burden, the fundamental physical reality of the marriage has been destabilized. Compassion fatigue is a biological reality, not a moral failure.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Remove Guilt from Exhaustion:** Validate the healthy partner's caretaker burnout. Remind them that needing a physical break does not mean they love their spouse any less.
2. **Externalize the Illness:** Advise the couple to mentally separate the disease from the person. The illness is a third entity in the room that they are both fighting against together.
3. **Seek Respite Care:** Strongly suggest the couple utilize external support systems (family, friends, or professional caregivers) to give the caretaker guaranteed hours of physical rest each week.
