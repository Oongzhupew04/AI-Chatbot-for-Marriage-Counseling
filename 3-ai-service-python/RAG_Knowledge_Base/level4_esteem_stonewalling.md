# Maslow Level: Esteem Needs
## Sub-Category: Stonewalling and Silent Treatment
## Related Survey Variables: Communication / Respect

**Psychological Context:**
Stonewalling occurs when a partner completely shuts down, refuses to speak, or gives the "silent treatment" for days after a conflict. While it may stem from being emotionally overwhelmed, using silence to punish a partner is deeply disrespectful and attacks the partner's Esteem by making them feel invisible and worthless.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
The AI must differentiate between a healthy "time-out" and toxic stonewalling. A time-out has a clear end time; stonewalling is indefinite emotional abandonment. 

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Break the Silence:** Advise the stonewalling partner that refusing to speak is destructive. They must learn to say, "I am too overwhelmed to talk right now, but I still love you, and we will talk tomorrow at 6 PM."
2. **Self-Soothe:** Encourage the stonewaller to practice self-soothing techniques (deep breathing, walking) during conflicts so they do not become so flooded that they shut down entirely.
3. **Stop Chasing:** Advise the partner receiving the silent treatment to stop "chasing" or begging for a response, as this feeds the power imbalance. They should state they are ready to talk when the partner is, and then focus on their own well-being.
