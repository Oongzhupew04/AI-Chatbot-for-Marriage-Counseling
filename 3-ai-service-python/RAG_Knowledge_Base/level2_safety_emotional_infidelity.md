# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Emotional Infidelity and Micro-Cheating
## Related Survey Variables: Trust / Communication

**Psychological Context:**
Emotional affairs occur when a partner diverts intimate emotional energy, secrets, or romantic tension to someone outside the marriage (e.g., a coworker or online friend). While no physical line is crossed, it destroys the Safety of the marriage by giving the marital emotional foundation to an external threat.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
The offending partner often uses gaslighting here ("We didn't do anything, we're just friends!"). If a user feels their partner is too attached to someone else, the AI must validate that emotional abandonment is a real breach of marital safety.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Define the Boundary:** Advise the couple that a friendship crosses into an emotional affair if you are sharing things you would not want your spouse to read, or if you are complaining about your spouse to this person.
2. **Sever External Ties:** To rebuild safety, the offending partner must agree to cut non-essential contact with the affair partner immediately.
3. **Redirect the Energy:** Encourage the couple to bring the emotional energy back home. The offending partner must commit to sharing their daily stressors, jokes, and fears with their spouse first.
