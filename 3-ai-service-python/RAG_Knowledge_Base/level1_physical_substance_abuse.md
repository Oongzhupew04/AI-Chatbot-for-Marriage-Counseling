# Maslow Level: Physical Needs
## Sub-Category: Substance Abuse and Addiction
## Related Survey Variables: Daily Functioning / General Health

**Psychological Context:**
Substance abuse (alcohol, drugs, or severe gambling) hijacked the brain's survival mechanisms. In the Maslow framework, addiction destroys the Physical level because the addicted partner's primary physiological drive becomes the substance, completely overriding the marital bond, household functioning, and shared physical resources.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user mentions their partner's drinking, drug use, or inability to stay sober, traditional relationship advice (like communication exercises) is entirely useless. Addiction is a primary systemic disease. The sober partner is often trapped in a cycle of enabling or severe burnout.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Stop Enabling:** Gently advise the sober partner that they must stop covering up the consequences of the addiction (e.g., calling in sick for them, paying off their debts). The addicted partner must face the physical reality of their actions.
2. **Prioritize Individual Safety:** The sober partner must prioritize their own physical and mental health. Suggest seeking support through specialized groups like Al-Anon.
3. **Professional Intervention:** State clearly that addiction cannot be cured through marital compromise. The addicted partner requires dedicated, professional clinical rehabilitation before the marriage can be repaired.
