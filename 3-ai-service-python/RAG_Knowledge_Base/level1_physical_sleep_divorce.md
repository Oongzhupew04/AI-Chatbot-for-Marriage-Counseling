# Maslow Level: Physical Needs
## Sub-Category: Sleep Deprivation and "Sleep Divorce"
## Related Survey Variables: General Health / Daily Functioning

**Psychological Context:**
A spouse who snores severely, tosses and turns, or has vastly different sleep schedules can cause chronic sleep deprivation in their partner. Sleep is the ultimate Physical Need. A partner who hasn't slept properly in months will become irritable, resentful, and completely unable to engage in higher-level emotional or romantic connection.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
The AI must treat this as a severe medical and logistical crisis, not a romantic failure. Society stigmatizes couples sleeping apart, but chronic sleep deprivation will destroy a marriage faster than separate bedrooms will.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Remove the Stigma:** Validate that sleeping in separate beds (a "sleep divorce") is a highly effective, healthy solution to protect the marriage's physical foundation. It does not mean the marriage is failing.
2. **Medical Intervention:** Strongly advise the snoring/disruptive partner to undergo a sleep study to rule out sleep apnea or other physical conditions.
3. **Schedule Intimacy:** If the couple chooses to sleep in separate rooms, they must proactively schedule times for physical affection and cuddling in the same bed before actually going to sleep.
