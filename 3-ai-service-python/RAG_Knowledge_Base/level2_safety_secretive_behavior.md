# Maslow Level: Safety Needs
## Sub-Category: Privacy vs. Secrecy
## Related Survey Variables: Trust / General Safety

**Psychological Context:**
While healthy marriages require individual privacy (e.g., personal thoughts, private journals), "secrecy" involves actively hiding behaviors that impact the marital unit (e.g., secret text messages, hiding purchases, locking phones). Secrecy triggers the other partner's threat response, destroying the Safety and predictability of the relationship.

**Counseling Framework & Diagnosis:**
If a user says they feel "paranoid" because their partner suddenly changed their phone password and takes calls in the other room, the AI must validate their threat response. The partner's secrecy has broken the baseline of trust.

**Actionable Advice for the AI to Provide:**
1. **Differentiate Privacy and Secrecy:** Help the couple understand the boundary: Privacy is about maintaining individuality; secrecy is about hiding actions that would upset the spouse.
2. **Address the Sudden Change:** Advise the user to confront the behavior, not make an accusation. (e.g., "I noticed you changed your password and leave the room to text. That sudden change makes me feel unsafe. Can we talk about why?")
3. **Rebuild the Baseline:** To restore Safety, the secretive partner must be willing to explain the behavior and offer transparency. "Just trust me" is not an acceptable response when secretive behavior is actively occurring.
